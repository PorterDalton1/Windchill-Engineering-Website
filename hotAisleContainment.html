Understanding

Hot Aisle Containment

Hot Aisle Containment (HAC) is a system designed to isolate hot air within the return aisle and efficiently direct it back to the cooling units. This is typically achieved using aisle-end doors and vertical walls above the server cabinets. By preventing conditioned air from mixing with the warmer air, HAC ensures that only the hottest air is recirculated, thereby optimizing the cooling unit's performance. The ability to handle higher temperatures while maintaining efficiency is a key advantage of Hot Aisle Containment.

Additionally, a Hot Aisle Containment configuration effectively transforms the general working area into a cold aisle, improving the overall comfort of the environment. In data centers, HAC eliminates hot spots, reduces the need for extensive cooling hardware, allows for an increase in cooling setpoints, and can eliminate the requirement for additional humidity control. These efficiencies typically result in a return on investment (ROI) of three years or less.
